stressed.

Stress is your body’s natural reaction to demand. Simply put it, your body’s “fight or flight” alarm. Usually, stress encourages you to get out of danger situations or encourage you to remove your source of stress like work. Sometimes though, your “fight or flight” system doesn’t shut off, and that’s when it becomes a problem.

People who were diagnosed as stressed mostly answered:
  • Anxiety
  • Restlessness
  • Frustration
  • Inability to focus
  • Irritability
  • Irregular sleeping patterns
  • Mood swings
  • Irregular eating patterns
  • High workload

Steps you can take to manage stress

Take short breaks
Practice meditation/mindfulness or yoga
Engage in hobbies to express stress positively
Engage in hobbies for recreation

{% elif result is equalto "Burnout" %}

You are probably experiencing burnout.

Burnout is kind of like feeling high level stress and fatigue ALL AT ONCE accompanied by regret or extreme frustration. It is a cycle of negative emotions, which is an indicator that you may no longer see the point in continuing what you’re doing. Rest is very much needed at this point.

Symptoms of burnout include:

Apathy
Withdrawal
Despair
Disillusionment
Depression
Self-doubt
Negative outlook
Change in routine
Substance abuse
Escaping responbilities

{% elif result is equalto "Fatigue" %}

You are probably experiencing fatigue.

Simply put, fatigue is being in the constant state of physical exhaustion. It is your body’s natural cry for much needed rest or time to recover and think.

Symptoms of fatigue include:

Inability to focus
Lack of energy and motivation
Substance abuse
Lack of sleep/irregular sleeping patterns
Irregular eating habits
Medication
Excess/lack of physical activities
Chest pain
Shortness of breath
Irregular heartbeat
Feeling of passing out
Abdominal, back, pelvic pain
Extreme – headache + bleeding
Restlessness
Short memory and attention span
Sluggish, slow reaction, micro sleep
Depression
Irritability
indecisiveness
