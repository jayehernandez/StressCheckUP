Facts

Stress, fatigue, and burnout; we’ve all experienced them at one point. Often times, they can be mistaken for each other, and for good reason since they’re closely assosciated. However, there are stark differences between the three.

Stress

Stress if your body’s natural reaction to physical and emotional demand. Otherwise put, it’s your body’s alarm system to a certain stimuli through hormones such as adrenaline. It is important to be noted that stress comes from you, not the environment. Stress is important because it keeps us alert, but if stress becomes unmanaged, that’s when stress becomes harmful.


There are actually four types of stress:
Survival
“fight or flight”, your body’s reaction to danger
Internal
This is stress caused by internal stimuli (thoughts, emotions, etc.); this can be the addicting type of stress
Environmental
This is stress caused by external factors like noise, crowds, pressure from work
Fatigue and Overwork
This is the type of stress that leads to more severe conditions like fatigue and burnout. This can be a result of overworking, lack of lifestyle management, and lack of rest.

Symptoms of Stress

  • Anxiety
  • Restlessness
  • Frustration
  • Inability to focus
  • Irritability
  • Irregular sleeping patterns
  • Mood swings
  • Irregular eating patterns
  • High workload

Fatigue

Simply put, fatigue is being in the constant state of physical exhaustion. It is your body’s natural cry for much needed rest or time to recover and think.

Symptoms of Fatigue

  • Exhaustion
  • Feeling of Fainting
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Inability to focus
  • Sluggishness
  • Excess/lack of Physical Actvity
  • Lack of Motivation
  • Irregular Sleeping Patterns

Burnout

Burnout is kind of like feeling high level stress and fatigue ALL AT ONCE accompanied by regret or extreme frustration. It is a cycle of negative emotions, which is an indicator that you may no longer see the point in continuing what you’re doing. Rest is very much needed at this point.

Symptoms of Burnout

  • Depression
  • Lack of Motivation
  • Exhaustion
  • Inability to focus
  • Irregular Sleeping Patterns
  • Fraustration
  • Lack of Control
  • Despair
  • Overwhelmed
