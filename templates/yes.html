{% if result is equalto "Burnout" %}

You are probably experiencing
burnout

Burnout is kind of like feeling high level stress and fatigue all at once accompanied by regret or extreme frustration. It is a cycle of negative emotions, which is an indicator that you may no longer see the point in continuing what you’re doing. Rest is very much needed at this point.

People who were diagnosed as burned out mostly answered:

  • Depression
  • Lack of motivation
  • Exhaustion
  • Inability to focus
  • Irregular sleeping patterns
  • Frustration
  • Lack of control
  • Despair
  • Overwhelmed

Steps you can take to manage burnout

Prioritizing tasks
Maximizing positive physical and mental activity
Regulating sleep and getting adequate rest
Staying away from alcohol and caffeine
Seek medical help or counseling if burnout becomes severe
Learn more about burnout here

If burnout becomes unmanageable, you can always visit the University Health Services and UP Office of Counseling and Guidance.

{% elif result is equalto "Stressed" %}

You are probably
stressed

Stress is your body’s natural reaction to demand. Simply put it, your body’s “fight or flight” alarm. Usually, stress encourages you to get out of danger situations or encourage you to remove your source of stress like work. Sometimes though, your “fight or flight” system doesn’t shut off, and that’s when it becomes a problem.

People who were diagnosed as stressed mostly answered:

  • Anxiety
  • Restlessness
  • Frustration
  • Inability to focus
  • Irritability
  • Irregular sleeping patterns
  • Mood swings
  • Irregular eating patterns
  • High workload

Steps you can take to manage stress

Take short breaks
Practice meditation/mindfulness or yoga
Engage in hobbies to express stress positively
Engage in hobbies for recreation
Learn more about stress here

If stress becomes unmanageable, you can always visit the University Health Services and UP Office of Counseling and Guidance.

UP OCG: 929-5835
{% elif result is equalto "Fatigue" %}

You are probably experiencing
fatigue

Simply put, fatigue is being in the constant state of physical exhaustion. It is your body’s natural cry for much needed rest or time to recover and think.

People who were diagnosed as fatigued mostly answered:

  • Exhaustion
  • Feeling of fainting
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Inability to focus
  • Sluggishness
  • Excess/lack of physical activity
  • Lack of motivation
  • Irregular sleeping patterns

Steps you can take to manage fatigue

Regulate sleep
Get enough rest
Participate in mindfulness or meditation exercises
Take supplements as needed or prescribed
Consult your doctor for more severe symptoms or pain
Learn more about fatigue here

If fatigue becomes unmanageable, you can always visit the University Health Services and UP Office of Counseling and Guidance.
